The Urban Infrastructure Lab (UIL) at the University of Washington, under the direction of Professor Jan Whittington, brings together students and faculty across numerous disciplines with a shared interest in the planning, governance, finance, design, development, economics, and environmental effects of infrastructure. Collectively, our interests span the systems critical to economic and social well-being, such as energy, water, health, transportation, education, and communications. Across these sectors, our studies integrate empirical and applied methods of research to discover the means to obtain long-run objectives, such as decarbonization, resilience, and information security, through decisions made today.

Current Research Projects

Carbon Smart and Resilient Capital Investment Planning

A methodology for captial investment planning that assists municipalities in selecting and prioritizing resilient, low carbon infrastructure for investment. This methodology is being produced for the World Bank.

Municipal Open Data and Privacy

An examination of and and policy recommendations for municipal open data, considering the public interest in privacy and social justice, as well as efficiency and convenience. This research is supported by a grant from the Berkeley Center for Law and Technology at the University of California and Microsoft.

Economic Analysis of Bridge Reconstructions

A transaction cost economic analysis of nationally-recognized bridge reconstruction projects, in partnership with the University of California, Berkeley, and Arup.

UW Solar

A student-led movement to expand solar power investment and support student research in cyber-physical systems at the University of Washington, known as UW-Solar. The first solar installation from this group was completed atop the Mercer A Apartment building in the spring of 2014. This project was supported by the University of Washington Campus Sustainability Fund, University of Washington Housing and Food Services, and the Bonneville Environmental Foundation.

Husky Sustainable Storms

A student-led project to design and build a stormwater bio-retention facility along the western entrance to the University of Washington campus. Known as Husky Sustainable Storms, this project is supported by the University of Washington Campus Sustainability Fund.
